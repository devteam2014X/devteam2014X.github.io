Abstract

Current research on paid search highlights its ability to aid consumers search process and is considered a highly effective marketing activity. Yet, research is focused towards the main search engines and does not account for one major decision every marketer faces. When crafting a paid search campaign, marketers need to decide whether they should solely rely on the search engine or if they should include so-called search partners (e.g. T-Online.de, web.de) to enhance their marketing reach. This paper presents a well-controlled field experiment using regression analysis to investigate whether the use of search partners can be beneficial for bricks-and-mortar retailers. Results indicate that paid search advertising budgets could be allocated more efficiently by introducing search partners as increased competition for attention on search partner websites decreases the likelihood of consumers clicking on a paid ad. However, behavior on the subsequent website does not seem to be influenced at all. Due to the pay-per-click billing mechanism the combination of a decrease in likelihood of clicking on an ad combined with no significant behavioral differences on the advertisers’ website is highly beneficial as it allows marketers to increase campaign reach at no additional costs. Consequently, bricks-and-mortar retailers should consider including search partners when crafting paid search campaigns.
