Abstract

Current research on paid search highlights its ability to enhance online as well as offline sales but is limited to online players and multi-channel firms. Using a controlled field experiment, we investigated whether paid search can increase sales for bricks-and-mortar-only retailers that mainly rely on informational websites to reach consumers. Our results suggest that paid search indeed increases the number of potential customers by enhancing the reach of online marketing initiatives. Yet, using a differences-in-differences analysis, our findings also show that paid search fails to increase offline sales. Local store customers primarily use paid search as a navigational shortcut to the retailer’s website which they would have been just as likely to reach without being exposed to paid search. Consequently, bricks-and-mortar-only retailers should approach paid search cautiously: whilst it can enhance the reach of marketing initiatives, it seems to have little effect on improving offline purchases.
